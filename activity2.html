Biogas Generation System for Dairy Farm

Source of Energy:

The primary source of energy in a biogas generation system is organic biomass, mainly cattle manure, crop residues, and other biodegradable wastes. In a dairy farm with 100 cattle, the daily manure collected provides a continuous and renewable feedstock rich in organic matter suitable for anaerobic digestion.

Conversion Process:

The process occurs in an anaerobic digester, where microorganisms break down organic matter in the absence of oxygen. This biological conversion produces biogas (a mixture of methane and carbon dioxide) and digestate (a nutrient-rich slurry). The produced gas is then purified through scrubbers and dryers to remove impurities like hydrogen sulfide and moisture.

Output / Utilization:

The main output is biogas, which can be used for cooking, lighting, water heating, or power generation through a biogas engine or generator. The digestate serves as an excellent organic fertilizer, enhancing soil fertility.

Real-World Application Relevance:

Biogas plants on dairy farms help manage waste sustainably, reduce methane emissions, and lower energy costs. They promote circular resource use — converting waste into energy and fertilizer — supporting rural energy independence and contributing to clean energy and climate action goals (SDG 7 & SDG 13).
